Au revoir, Michael!

Michael Mao

I had the pleasure of working with Michael for a long time, which began many decades ago around this time of the year. I gleaned from his breadth of knowledge of art history/architecture, love of music as well as his infectious enthusiasm for life (and wine!) and appreciation for everyone that walked through our doors. He set the tone for the VRC as being a place of welcome and excellence in our service and work, with a smile and then some. His legacy lives on in those us still here and in those graduated students who have gone on to establish their own careers but doubtless carry fond memories of Michael and his unique contribution to their time at UBC. – Jane Young

Arts Digital Collections: PIX

""Database by christophe.benoit74 is licensed under CC BY-NC-ND 2.0


The VRC maintains a large database of images and other digital assets called PIX. Please access it here...".

To maintain a dedicated image database that reflects current teaching needs and research interests of AHVA, the open-source program Omeka was implemented in June 2015 to supplant the Quest Image Gallery (MDID2). In early 2020, all VRC digital data were successfully migrated to Arts Digital Collections (WordPress) maintained by Arts ISIT for storage and access. Digitized visual materials are regularly updated and accessible in an intranet environment. The University Data Centre, overseen by Arts ISIT, provides long-term storage needs for all VRC digital assets in a secure environment.
